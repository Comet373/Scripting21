Edmonton is the capital of Alberta and is located on the North Saskatchewan River, near the geographical center of the entire province. It is commonly referred to as the “Gateway to the North”, and it is strategically located on an economic divide between the highly productive farmlands of central Alberta and the rich in resources northern hinterland. Edmonton is also home to mountainous region called the Colombia mountains, the ecozone of this landmass is called the Taiga Cordillera Ecozone which is home to many mountains, rivers, wetlands and mixed forests such as deciduous forests and coniferous forests. The land in and around Edmonton is generally flat to gently rolling with some ravines and deep river valleys located all over. Due to having mostly flat ground Edmonton is very safe from floods and snow blockages unlike Calgary which suffers from it often. Also due to its location and weather Edmonton is often referred to as the most northerly city in North America.
